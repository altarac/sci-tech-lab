Part D: Chemical Equilibrium

 

Question 11

 

For years, scientists tried to produce fertilizers in order to increase crop production as well as food quality. Finally, in the early 1900s, two German chemists developed the process for making ammonia from nitrogen and hydrogen gas:

 

N2(g) + 3 H2(g) ↔ 2 NH3(g)

 

At a constant temperature, 0.30 mol of N2 and 0.30 mol of H2 are placed in a sealed 1.0 L flask and allowed to reach equilibrium. At equilibrium, the 1 L flask contains 0.10 mol of ammonia (NH3).

 

a)       Determine the equilibrium constant, Keq,for this reaction.

 

b)       How could changing the pressure of the reaction vessel be used to favour the production of ammonia? Explain your answer using Le Châtelier’s Principle.
