Question 7

The nitrogen cycle is an important nutrient cycle that occurs in ecosystems. During the nitrogen cycle, the nitrogen that is in the atmosphere is converted to a form that can be used by plants.

 

During another part of the nitrogen cycle, nitrogen dioxide (NO2) dissolves in rainwater in the presence of oxygen gas to form nitric acid (HNO3). The balanced chemical equation for this reaction is shown below.

 

4 NO2(g) + 2 H2O(l) + O2(g) ® 4 HNO3(aq) (reaction 1)

 

A chemist is studying the energy changes for this reaction and collects the following information:

 

2 H2(g) + O2(g)                     ®     2 H2O(g)                             DH = -483.6 kJ

N2(g) + 3 O2(g) + H2(g)    ®     2 HNO3(aq)                 DH = -207.0 kJ

2 NO2(g)                                 ®     2 NO(g) + O2(g)           DH = -112.8 kJ

H2O(l)                                       ®     H2O(g)                                 DH = +44.0 kJ

N2(g) + O2(g)                         ®     2 NO(g)                               DH = +180.6 kJ

 

Determine the heat of reaction, ∆H, for the dissolving of nitrogen dioxide in rain water (reaction 1).
