Question 18

 

The following is the simplified reaction in which glucose (C6H12O6) is converted to energy for use in the body.

 

C6H12O6(aq) + 6 O2(g) ® 6 CO2(g) + 6 H2O(l) + 2870 kJ

 

In reality, this reaction takes place in cells a few molecules at a time. It can be studied in the laboratory by dissolving 108.0 g of glucose in 150.0 mL of water and bubbling oxygen through the solution. It takes 10.0 minutes to oxidize all the glucose.

 

How long will it take to oxidize the same amount of glucose (108.0 g), if the amount of oxygen is doubled?
