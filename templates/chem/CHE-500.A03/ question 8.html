Question 8

 

In order for humans to be able to live on the planet Mars, a life-support system similar to Earth’s would need to exist. Earth’s atmosphere contains a mixture of many gases, of which the principle components are: nitrogen, oxygen, argon and other trace gases.

 

To recreate our atmosphere, a scientist connects three one-litre flasks containing the gases indicated in the diagram below. The flasks are connected to each other by a stopcock. In order to verify whether this gas mixture corresponds to earth’s atmosphere, the scientist must determine the percentage (mole fraction) of oxygen (O2) in this mixture.

 

·      Flask one contains argon (Ar) gas at 150 kPa.

·      Flask two contains nitrogen (N2) gas at 75 kPa.

·      Flask three contains oxygen (O2) at 106 kPa.

 

The valve is opened, allowing all three gases to mix.

 

What is the percentage (mole fraction) of oxygen (O2) in this mixture?
