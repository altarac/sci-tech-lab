Question 24

 

In a laboratory, the three following solutions of acids are available:

 

Solution 1:       100 mL of hydrofluoric acid solution, HF, with a concentration of 0.010 mol/L and a pH of 2.60;

 

Solution 2:       100 mL of acetic acid solution, HCH3COO, with a concentration of 0.10 mol/L and an acidity constant, Ka, of 1.8 ´ 10-5;

 

Solution 3:       100 mL of chlorous acid, HClO2, with a concentration of 0.0050 mol/L and a concentration of hydronium ions, H+, of 3.73 ´ 10-3 mol/L.

 

Which of these acids is the strongest?
