Question 18

 

Graphite is a solid form of carbon. When graphite is added to a container that is under high pressure and high temperature that contains carbon dioxide gas, CO2, carbon monoxide, CO, is formed.

 

Graphite is added to a 0.50 L container containing 0.41 moles of carbon dioxide. At equilibrium, there was found to be 0.54 mol of CO.

 

The equation is shown below:

 

C(s) + CO2(g)  2 CO(g)

 

Determine the equilibrium constant, Kc, for this reaction.
