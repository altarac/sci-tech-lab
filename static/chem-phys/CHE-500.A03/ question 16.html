Question 16

 

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O) to produce glucose (C6H12O6) and oxygen (O2). A plant produces 100 mL of glucose solution. The concentration of this solution is 0.83 mol/L.

 

The following balanced equation represents this reaction:

 

6CO2 + 6H2O ® C6H12O6 + 6O2

 

What volume of carbon dioxide at SATP must the plant have absorbed to have produced this amount of glucose?
