The normal pH of blood in the arteries of the human body is 7.35-7.45. If the pH is altered, various problems can arise. Cells cannot function properly if blood pH is below pH 6.8 or above pH 8.0.

 

The pH of the blood is controlled by a buffering system. Part of the buffering system involves the equilibrium reaction that takes place when carbonic acid (H2CO3) dissociates to produce bicarbonate ions (HCO3-) and hydrogen ions (H+).

 

H2CO3(aq)  ↔  H+(aq)  +  HCO3-(aq)   Ka = 4.15 x 10-7
